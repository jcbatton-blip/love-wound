## THE 6 LOVE WOUNDS

### 1. **"If You Love Me, You'll Hurt Me"**
**The Download:** Love = Pain  
**The Pattern:** You're attracted to people who are emotionally or physically abusive. When someone treats you well, you feel nothing. The "spark" only shows up when there's danger.

**Childhood Imprint:**
- Dad hit Mom (or you)
- Parents fought violently
- Love was expressed through rage

**Adult Manifestation:**
- You pick partners who yell, demean, or hit
- Calm, stable people feel "boring"
- You mistake intensity for passion

**The Mirror Moment:**
"If you don't hurt me, you don't really love me."

---

### 2. **"If You Love Me, You'll Medicate"**
**The Download:** Love = Numbing Together  
**The Pattern:** You're attracted to drinkers, drug users, workaholics, or any form of escape artist. Sobriety feels like rejection.

**Childhood Imprint:**
- Dad was an alcoholic (or Mom)
- Parents used substances to cope
- Love was expressed through shared numbing

**Adult Manifestation:**
- You pick partners who drink/use heavily
- Sober partners feel "uptight" or "no fun"
- You enable their addiction because it feels like love

**The Mirror Moment:**
"If you don't medicate with me, you don't really love me."

---

### 3. **"If You Love Me, You'll Leave"**
**The Download:** Love = Abandonment  
**The Pattern:** You're attracted to people who are emotionally unavailable, physically absent, or already taken. When someone shows up consistently, you lose interest.

**Childhood Imprint:**
- Dad was never home (worked constantly, traveled, left)
- Mom was emotionally absent (depressed, checked out)
- Love was expressed through absence

**Adult Manifestation:**
- You pick partners who are always "busy"
- You're attracted to married people or long-distance relationships
- When someone is available, you feel smothered

**The Mirror Moment:**
"If you're here, you don't really love me."

---

### 4. **"If You Love Me, You'll Need Me"**
**The Download:** Love = Being Needed  
**The Pattern:** You're attracted to broken people who need fixing. You're the savior, the therapist, the parent. When someone is self-sufficient, you feel useless.

**Childhood Imprint:**
- Mom was a martyr (sacrificed everything for you)
- Dad was helpless (you had to take care of him)
- Love was expressed through caretaking

**Adult Manifestation:**
- You pick partners with addiction, mental illness, or chaos
- You feel valued only when you're solving their problems
- Healthy, independent people feel threatening

**The Mirror Moment:**
"If you don't need me, you don't really love me."

---

### 5. **"If You Love Me, You'll Be Silent"**
**The Download:** Love = Silence  
**The Pattern:** You're attracted to people who don't communicate, don't express emotions, or shut down during conflict. When someone wants to talk, you feel overwhelmed.

**Childhood Imprint:**
- Mom never spoke up (passive, quiet, invisible)
- Dad raged silently (suppressed anger, cold shoulder)
- Love was expressed through silence

**Adult Manifestation:**
- You pick partners who won't talk about feelings
- You avoid conflict at all costs
- When someone wants emotional intimacy, you withdraw

**The Mirror Moment:**
"If you speak, you don't really love me."

---

### 6. **"If You Love Me, You'll Control Me"**
**The Download:** Love = Control  
**The Pattern:** You're attracted to people who are anxious, controlling, or micromanaging. When someone gives you freedom, you feel unloved.

**Childhood Imprint:**
- Mom was anxious/controlling (managed everything)
- Dad was domineering (his way or the highway)
- Love was expressed through control

**Adult Manifestation:**
- You pick partners who tell you what to do
- You mistake jealousy for love
- When someone trusts you, you feel abandoned

**The Mirror Moment:**
"If you don't control me, you don't really love me."

## PATTERN RECOGNITION WORKSHEET

**Instructions:** Answer these questions honestly. Your first instinct is usually correct.

### Part 1: Your Childhood

1. How did your parents express love to each other?
2. What was the emotional climate of your home? (calm, chaotic, silent, explosive)
3. Who was the "strong" parent? Who was the "weak" parent?
4. What did you learn about love by watching them?
5. What did you have to do to feel loved as a child?

### Part 2: Your Patterns

1. Describe your last 3 relationships in one sentence each.
2. What do they have in common?
3. When do you feel the "spark"? (What behaviors or traits turn you on?)
4. When do you lose interest? (What makes someone feel "boring"?)
5. What do your friends say about your dating patterns?

### Part 3: The Mirror

1. Which of the 6 Love Wounds resonates most?
2. What was your immediate reaction when you read it? (resistance, recognition, tears)
3. Can you see the connection between your childhood and your adult patterns?
4. What would it mean if you stopped running this pattern?

## NEXT STEPS GUIDE

**What to Do After You Identify Your Wound**

### Step 1: Sit With It
Don't try to fix it immediately. Just notice it. Watch how it shows up in your life. This is the awareness phase.

### Step 2: Stop Running the Pattern
The next time you feel the "spark" with someone, pause. Ask yourself: "Is this my wound talking, or is this real attraction?"

### Step 3: Practice New Responses
When someone treats you differently than your wound expects, don't run. Stay. Let yourself feel uncomfortable. This is how you rewire.

### Step 4: Get Support
This work is hard to do alone. Consider:
- **Mirror Session** ($350) - 90-minute deep dive into your specific pattern
- **12-Week Container** ($5,000) - Intensive coaching to rewrite the pattern
- **Group Container** ($350/month) - Weekly support with others doing the work

# Insights from "Grinnin' Like a Jackass Eatin' Briars"

## Core Themes
*   **"Caddywonked from the get-go":** A feeling of being out of place or "wrong" from the start (adoption, unwed mother).
*   **The "Imprint" Origin:** Adopted into a strict Southern home (Emily Post + Bible vs. Alcohol + Farming). This dichotomy likely formed the early "Love Wound" code.
*   **The Journey:** From a 700-acre peanut farm in Georgia to NYC, Hong Kong, London, and finally Detroit.
*   **The Transformation:** "Insanity, Awareness, and Acceptance." This mirrors the "Wound -> Code -> Pattern" framework.
*   **Voice/Style:** "Warm and stark transparency," "laugh-out-loud humor," "Southern grit."
*   **Key Quote:** "Coaches people on what not to do." (Humility/Anti-Guru approach).

## Anecdotes to Weave In
*   **The "Delicatessen Case":** Being displayed for adoption like a cold cut. (Powerful metaphor for the "Rejection/Unworthiness" wound).
*   **"Piping in Sunshine":** The farm was so isolated they had to "pipe in sunshine." (Metaphor for isolation/loneliness).
*   **Global Perspective:** The shift from barefoot farm boy to international traveler adds authority and depth.

## Integration Plan
*   **About Page:** Use the "Caddywonked" opening to hook the reader. Contrast the "Emily Post" upbringing with the "Alcohol" reality to illustrate the *Imprint* concept (mixed messages about love).
*   **Philosophy:** Emphasize "Coaching on what *not* to do" – the anti-guru stance.
*   **Tone:** Infuse the copy with Southern idioms and raw honesty. Less "clinical," more "front porch truth."
